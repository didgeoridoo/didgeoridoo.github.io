Free markets work

Unfortunately, despite the unprecedented level of freedom the world has been afforded in the age of the Internet, there is still no free market for forecasts. This is partially as a result of third party interference and regulation. We believe that individuals, pundits, and media sources, among others, should be rewarded for accurate forecasts, and be penalized for being incorrect. That is how to create an efficient market.

Enter Augur, a decentralized prediction market platform for the 21st century, and beyond.

Instead of living in a world where punditry and rhetoric are rewarded, we envision a world where those who forecast honestly, and accurately, are the ones who are valued, not those with the loudest voices. When a pundit announces that "the claims of climate change alarmists will be proven wrong in the next year", that pundit, or any of their viewers, may create an Augur market for this prediction. Thus, such debates will not be left to scientists to correct. Instead, anyone can have a stake in determining the truth by betting on what they believe will truly happen.

The potential applications of the Augur project are limitless. The platform has the potential to fundamentally change the ways that individual receive and verify "truth" by providing incentives for honesty in prediction-making.
